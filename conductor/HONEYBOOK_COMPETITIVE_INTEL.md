# HoneyBook Competitive Intelligence Report

> **Date**: 2026-03-29
> **Purpose**: Competitive analysis for Felmark positioning
> **Sources**: G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, BBB, Reddit, Software Advice, GetApp, Plutio, Fstoppers, TaskIP, Assembly, various comparison blogs

## 1. What HoneyBook Does Well (Consistently Praised)

### All-in-One Simplicity
- Combines proposals, contracts, invoicing, scheduling, and client communication in a single platform
- Users consistently praise the "one place for everything" value proposition
- Replaces 3-5 separate tools for solo freelancers

### Templates and Quick Setup
- Pre-built templates for contracts, proposals, invoices, and onboarding flows
- New users can be operational in 1-2 days (vs. weeks for Dubsado)
- "Smart files" combine multiple document types (proposal + contract + invoice) into one client-facing package

### Automation for Non-Technical Users
- Workflow automations for follow-ups, reminders, and status changes
- Users say automations make their businesses "appear more polished"
- Low barrier to entry -- no coding or complex configuration required

### Client Experience
- Branded client portal for organized communication
- Clients can view, sign, and pay in one flow
- Professional appearance that freelancers appreciate for client-facing work

### Integrations
- QuickBooks, Zapier, Calendly, Gmail, Google Calendar
- Zapier extends to hundreds of additional tools

### Mobile App
- Functional mobile app for on-the-go project management
- Mobile stopwatch for basic time tracking

## 2. What Users Complain About Most (Ranked by Frequency)

### Tier 1: Extremely Common Complaints

**A. Pricing / Price Hikes**
- February 2025: 89% increase on Starter plan ($19 -> $36/month)
- Essentials: $39 -> $59/month (51% increase)
- Premium: $79 -> $129/month (63% increase)
- Users report paying ~$250/year initially, then being charged $500+/year with no proportional feature improvement
- Transaction fees compound the problem: 2.9% + $0.25 per card transaction, 1.5% for ACH
- Processing 10x $1,000 invoices monthly = $292.50 in fees on top of subscription
- Quote from Reddit: "The price increase was so extreme... the value proportion of the new pricing is unjustified"

**B. Payment Processing**
- Slowest deposits on the market -- 2-3 business days for cards, 7-8 business days for ACH
- Square deposits next day; Stripe deposits in 2 days
- Cannot alter payment amounts once set
- Cannot split payments between different cards
- Instant deposit available but at additional fee
- US and Canada only -- international freelancers are locked out entirely
- HoneyBook branding appears on all client-facing payment documents

**C. Limited Customization / Flexibility**
- Automation limited to basic actions with one trigger per automation (Dubsado offers more)
- Reporting is surface-level -- no deep revenue analytics, no team performance metrics
- Project pipeline lacks adequate customization
- Fixed branding on documents (clients see "HoneyBook" on every invoice)
- Cannot force clients to complete every step of a file (creates legal risk when clients pay without signing contracts)

### Tier 2: Very Common Complaints

**D. No Data Export / Vendor Lock-in**
- No bulk data export -- files must be downloaded individually
- Users describe migration as intentionally difficult
- Trustpilot reviewer called it "slimy" -- designed to trap users
- This is a major pain point for anyone trying to leave

**E. Time Tracking Is Essentially Useless**
- Mobile stopwatch only -- no desktop timer
- Desktop requires manual time entry
- CRITICAL GAP: Tracked hours do NOT flow into invoices
- Freelancers billing hourly must use Toggl/Harvest separately, then manually rebuild invoice line items
- Time tracking exists as a checkbox feature, not a real workflow

**F. Customer Support Deterioration**
- Response times vary from hours to days
- Weekend support essentially nonexistent
- Users report spending 3+ weeks trying to reach a live person
- AI chatbot gates access to human support
- No phone support available
- Issues not escalated properly

### Tier 3: Significant but Less Frequent

**G. Project Management Gaps**
- No Kanban boards, Gantt charts, task dependencies, or timeline views
- Designed for booking/intake, not multi-phase project delivery
- Users layer Asana or Monday.com ($11-27/month) on top
- Task management has no description field (title only)

**H. Client Portal Limitations**
- Clients cannot check project progress by individual task
- No deliverable-specific feedback capability
- Clients cannot download organized files without emailing
- Portal handles booking and payment only, not ongoing collaboration

**I. Mobile vs. Desktop Parity**
- Email editing limited on mobile
- Reporting features missing from mobile
- Desktop features significantly more capable

## 3. Common Themes in Negative Reviews (by Platform)

### Trustpilot (Harshest Reviews)
- "Overstyled mess where nothing makes sense"
- "Very buggy -- team takes weeks to resolve issues"
- "Slimy" data practices preventing easy migration
- "Significantly increased prices" with no corresponding value
- Surprise processing fees despite paying monthly subscription

### BBB Complaints (Most Severe)
- Client disputes cause HoneyBook to remove ALL money from contracts without warning, held for 90 days
- One user lost a $29,500 contract after forced refund + 2-week runaround, nearly closing their business
- Accounts frozen based on fraudulent information with no user notification
- Instant payments turned off (perceived as retaliatory)
- No account statements for monthly periods -- standard bookkeeping impossible

### G2 / Capterra (Most Balanced)
- Steep learning curve contradicts marketing claims of simplicity
- Integration issues complicating workflow and data export
- Feature issues with reporting, automations, and project date syncing
- Pricing "steep" and "may deter potential customers"

### Reddit / Forums
- Frustration with 89% price hike driving mass exodus
- Freelance photographers and videographers finding the price unjustified for "basic CRM needs"
- Users actively sharing alternatives and migration strategies

## 5. Pricing Complaints Summary

### Current Pricing (Post-Hike)

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | $36/month | $29/month |
| Essentials | $59/month | $49/month |
| Premium | $129/month | $109/month |

### Transaction Fees (On Top of Subscription)
- Credit/Debit: 2.9% + $0.25 per transaction
- ACH: 1.5%
- Processing time: 2-3 days (card), 7-8 days (ACH)

### Core Pricing Grievances
1. **89% hike with no proportional value** -- the single most cited reason for users exploring alternatives
2. **Double-dipping** -- subscription fee PLUS transaction fees on every payment
3. **Scaling penalty** -- the more invoices you send, the more fees eat into revenue
4. **Hidden costs** -- users surprised by processing fees after signing up
5. **Dispute fees** -- charged when clients file payment disputes
6. **No grandfathering** -- existing loyal users hit with same increases

## 6. Why Users Leave (Comparison Pain Points)

### To Dubsado
- More powerful automations (multiple triggers, complex workflows)
- More customization control for growing businesses
- Lower total cost for power users
- No forced third-party branding on documents

### To Plutio
- Built-in project management (Kanban, timelines, dependencies)
- Time tracking that actually connects to invoicing
- Client portal with progress tracking and deliverable feedback
- International payment support
- Better data export

### To Moxie
- Purpose-built for freelancers (not just creative event professionals)
- Integrated time tracking with invoice generation
- Better expense tracking
- More affordable at scale

### To Bonsai / AND.CO
- Better for hourly-billing freelancers
- Time tracking flows directly into invoices
- Tax preparation features
- International support

### Common Exit Triggers
1. Price hike announcement (February 2025 was a mass exodus trigger)
2. Outgrowing the platform (business scales, HoneyBook doesn't)
3. Needing real time tracking (hourly billers hit a wall)
4. International expansion (US/Canada payment lock-in)
5. Bad dispute experience (funds frozen, business nearly closed)
6. Data export frustration (realizing you're locked in)

## 7. Felmark Opportunity Map

Based on this analysis, HoneyBook's weaknesses map directly to Felmark opportunities:

| HoneyBook Weakness | Felmark Opportunity |
|---|---|
| Time tracking doesn't connect to invoicing | Build time-to-invoice as a core workflow |
| No real project management | Client workspaces with task management |
| US/Canada only payments | Design for international from day one |
| No data export / vendor lock-in | Open data, easy export (Markdown, JSON, plain text) |
| $36-129/month + transaction fees, 7-day trial only | **Free forever** plan with full starter toolkit; Pro at $12/month when you outgrow it |
| Slow payment deposits | Partner with faster payment processor |
| HoneyBook branding forced | White-label everything for Pro users |
| No desktop time tracking | Auto time tracking via Chrome extension (domain detection) |
| Surface-level reporting | Hours vs. budget dashboards per client |
| Mobile-first time tracking | Desktop-first (where freelancers actually work) |

### Felmark's Structural Advantages
1. **Free forever plan** -- HoneyBook gives you 7 days then demands $36/month. Felmark gives freelancers a real, permanent free tier with all the initial features to start their business automatically. No trial, no credit card gate, no "upgrade to unlock basics." You pay when you outgrow it, not before you've proven value.
2. **Chrome extension = zero friction** -- HoneyBook requires opening a separate app; Felmark lives in the browser where work happens
3. **Auto time tracking via domain detection** -- no manual entry, no stopwatch; just work and Felmark tracks which client you're working for
4. **Notes-first entry point** -- captures the "thinking" phase of freelance work that HoneyBook completely ignores
5. **No vendor lock-in by design** -- export everything, always
