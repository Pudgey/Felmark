# Assembly.com - Competitive Intelligence Report

**Prepared for:** Felmark competitive analysis
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Subject:** Assembly (formerly Copilot.app) - AI-Powered Client Portal & CRM Platform

---

## Company Overview

- **Founded:** 2020 (as Copilot; rebranded to Assembly in September 2025)
- **Founders:** Marlon Misra, Neil Raina
- **HQ:** New York City
- **Funding:** $10M Series A
- **User base:** 1,000+ businesses, 1M+ clients on the platform
- **Rebrand reason:** "Copilot" became unsearchable due to Microsoft Copilot, GitHub Copilot, etc.

---

## Target Market

**Primary:** Tech-enabled professional service firms — agencies, consultancies, accounting firms, law firms

**Specific verticals they target:**
- Accounting & Bookkeeping
- Marketing Agencies
- Consulting Firms
- Law Firms
- Real Estate / Property Management
- Startups
- Designers
- Freelancers (secondary — they position more for teams/firms than solos)

**Key positioning insight:** Assembly explicitly acknowledges that HoneyBook and Dubsado are better for solo freelancers, while Assembly "shines with larger teams" due to custom roles, client access permissions, and multi-user features. Their sweet spot is the 2-20 person service firm, not the individual freelancer.

## What Users Praise (Pros)

### From G2 (4.8 stars, ~294 reviews) and Product Hunt (5.0 stars, 10 reviews):

1. **Client portal experience** — 69% of G2 reviews highlight this as a standout. Users love the branded, professional portal clients interact with.

2. **All-in-one platform** — Consolidates messaging, files, billing, contracts, and tasks. Reduces tool sprawl.

3. **Clean, modern UI** — Frequently praised for being well-designed, intuitive, and visually polished.

4. **AI features** — The AI Assistant gets strong praise (19 mentions on Product Hunt) for drafting messages, summarizing threads, and prepping invoices.

5. **Quick setup** — 18% of G2 reviews specifically praise fast onboarding. 14-day trial with full access.

6. **Responsive customer support** — Described as "super responsive and a joy to work with." Team is active and ships improvements frequently.

7. **White-labeling quality** — Custom domains, branding, and badge removal make it feel like your own platform.

8. **Active development** — Users note the team is "always adding functionality and improving the platform." Frequent updates (multiple per week based on changelog).

9. **Integration flexibility** — Embed anything via iframe. Zapier/Make connectivity for 1,000+ apps.

### From Trustpilot (4.0 stars, 5 reviews):
- "Awesome customer support and amazing portal interface"
- "Speedy interface and access to client files and communication in one place"

## What Users Complain About (Cons & Pain Points)

### Critical Missing Features

1. **No native mobile app** — This is the single most-requested feature. Users want iOS/Android apps with push notifications and native widgets. The mobile web experience is described as confusing and requiring clients to manually "add to home screen" and enable notifications.

2. **No native time tracking** — Assembly has no built-in time tracker. Freelancers and agencies billing hourly must use a separate tool (Harvest, Toggl, etc.) or embed one.

3. **No native scheduling** — Relies entirely on embedded third-party calendars (Acuity, Calendly).

4. **No native reporting/analytics** — Beyond basic payment analytics, there is no reporting dashboard. Users must embed Looker Studio or Databox.

### Billing & Payments Issues

5. **International billing problems** — Canadian users billing in USD report significant friction. Payment collection system described as a "biggest shortfall" for international use cases.

6. **Limited invoice editing** — Users want more flexibility to customize and edit invoices after creation.

### Platform Limitations

7. **Expensive for what you get** — Multiple reviews call it "pricey" and "somewhat minimalistic" for the cost. The $39/mo Starter plan only gets 1 user and 50 clients. Solo freelancers find the pricing hard to justify vs. HoneyBook ($19/mo) or Bonsai.

8. **Limited customization depth** — Despite being "customizable," advanced customization is described as "tricky." Users want more control over portal layout and design.

9. **Automation reliability** — Several users report automations (especially tag-based triggers) failing silently without clear error messages.

10. **Limited reporting depth** — Users want better built-in analytics and dashboards rather than relying on embedded third-party tools.

11. **Learning curve for clients** — Some clients struggle with UX clarity — desire for "clearer buttons and iconography, especially surrounding messages and tasks."

12. **No multiple client dashboards** — Cannot create different dashboard views for different client segments (partially addressed in March 2026 update with homepage variations).

13. **Filtering and search** — Users wish for better ways to filter posts and find information without scrolling.

## Assembly vs. HoneyBook — How Assembly Positions Itself

Assembly has extensive comparison content on their blog targeting HoneyBook users. Key positioning:

### Assembly's Claimed Advantages Over HoneyBook

| Dimension | Assembly's Claim | HoneyBook's Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| **Scalability** | Built for growing teams/firms | Limits on users, automations, and reporting push you into higher tiers quickly |
| **Transaction fees** | Fees decrease on higher plans; surcharging option | Fixed rates across all tiers; costs add up at volume |
| **Client portal** | Full white-labeled branded portal with custom domain | Basic client portal, less customization |
| **Integrations** | API, Zapier, Make, custom apps, embeds | Limited to QuickBooks Online, fewer native integrations |
| **CRM depth** | Custom fields, multi-company clients, internal notes | Simpler CRM, less structured client data |
| **Team support** | Multiple users with custom roles and permissions | Single-user limitation on Starter plan |
| **AI features** | AI Assistant for drafts, summaries, task creation | No comparable AI feature |

### Where HoneyBook Actually Beats Assembly

| Dimension | HoneyBook Advantage | Assembly Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| **Price for solos** | Starts at $19/mo (Starter) | Starts at $39/mo (annual) |
| **Mobile app** | Native iOS and Android app | No native mobile app |
| **Scheduling** | Built-in scheduling tool | No native scheduling |
| **Templates** | Rich template library for proposals, contracts | Fewer ready-made templates |
| **Ease of setup** | Known for quick, easy onboarding | More complex setup for full configuration |
| **Freelancer focus** | Designed specifically for solos | Better suited for teams/firms |

### Assembly's Own Admission
Assembly's blog content acknowledges that HoneyBook and Dubsado "are excellent for freelancers" — they position themselves as the platform you graduate to when you outgrow those tools.

## Competitive Landscape Position

### Direct Competitors
- **HoneyBook** — Solo freelancers, event pros, creatives ($19-79/mo)
- **Dubsado** — Solo/small teams, highly customizable workflows ($20-40/mo)
- **Bonsai** — Freelancer-first, contracts/invoicing/tax ($25-79/mo)
- **ManyRequests** — Agency client portals ($99-399/mo)
- **SPP (Service Provider Pro)** — Agency portals with more analytics
- **Zendo** — Simpler portals at lower cost
- **17hats** — Small business all-in-one

### Assembly's Unique Position
Assembly occupies the "mid-market service firm" niche — more powerful than HoneyBook/Dubsado but less enterprise than Salesforce. Their differentiator is the white-labeled client portal as the centerpiece (not just a bolt-on feature).

## Implications for Felmark

### Gaps Felmark Could Exploit

1. **Assembly has no time tracking** — This is a core Felmark feature. Assembly users must bolt on Harvest/Toggl separately.

2. **Assembly has no native mobile experience** — Their most-requested feature. Felmark as a Chrome extension already has a different distribution model, but a PWA or mobile companion could be a differentiator.

3. **Assembly is expensive for freelancers** — $39/mo minimum (annual) for 1 user and 50 clients. Felmark's free tier with 3 workspaces directly undercuts this for solos.

4. **Assembly targets firms, not freelancers** — They explicitly position away from solo freelancers. Felmark owns that lane.

5. **Assembly has no notes/editor** — No Notion-style block editor, no rich note-taking. Their "notes" are just internal notes on CRM records. Felmark's editor-first approach is differentiated.

6. **No browser-native experience** — Assembly is a web app with a separate login. Felmark lives in the browser via Chrome extension (new tab override), which is fundamentally different distribution.

7. **International billing friction** — Assembly users in non-US markets report significant payment issues. If Felmark nails Stripe Connect internationally from day one, this is a wedge.

### Where Assembly is Strong (Felmark Should Note)

- White-labeled client portal is genuinely impressive and well-reviewed
- AI Assistant integration is forward-thinking and well-received
- Active development pace (multiple updates per week)
- Strong SEO/content marketing (their blog ranks for dozens of competitor comparison terms)
- 4.8 stars on G2 with ~300 reviews is serious social proof
