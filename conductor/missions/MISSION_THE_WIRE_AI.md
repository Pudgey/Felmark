# Mission: The Wire — AI-Powered Business Intelligence

**Created**: 2026-03-30
**Status**: Planning
**Milestone**: M2 (Pro feature)

---

## Goal

When a freelancer opens The Wire, they see 8-12 personalized business intelligence signals generated by Claude — rate benchmarks, pricing insights, industry trends, and client signals — all specific to their services, clients, and rates. No manual curation. No generic content. Fresh every 24 hours.

---

## The Problem

Freelancers price blind. They don't know if their rates are competitive, if their proposal structure wins deals, or if their niche is trending up or down. The tools that claim to provide this data are either:
- Too generic (Glassdoor/PayScale averages that don't reflect freelance reality)
- Too expensive (industry reports behind $500 paywalls)
- Too noisy (Reddit threads and Twitter posts with no signal)

The Wire solves this by turning the freelancer's own data into personalized intelligence via AI.

---

## How It Works

### Data Flow

```
User opens The Wire
  ↓
Check cache (localStorage, 24hr TTL)
  ↓ (cache miss or manual refresh)
Gather context from state:
  - Services (names, tiers, pricing)
  - Workspaces (clients, industries)
  - Rate ($95/hr or whatever they've set)
  - Pipeline (deal stages, values)
  - Recent activity (what they've been working on)
  ↓
POST /api/wire { context }
  ↓
System prompt + context → Claude Haiku
  ↓
Returns: JSON array of signal cards
  ↓
Cache response (24hr TTL)
  ↓
Render signal cards in The Wire UI
```

### Signal Types

| Type | Icon | Color | Example |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| `rate` | $ | green | "Your Brand Identity pricing ($4,800) is in the top 30% for your niche" |
| `trend` | ↗ | ember | "Demand for email sequence work increased this quarter" |
| `insight` | ◎ | blue | "Proposals with milestone payment schedules close 2x faster" |
| `signal` | ⚑ | purple | "Coaching businesses typically expand to social media after landing pages" |
| `alert` | ! | red | "Your App Onboarding project is 4 days overdue — similar projects average 3 weeks" |
| `benchmark` | ◆ | brown | "Your average deal size ($2,130) is above the design freelancer median ($1,800)" |

### System Prompt

```
You are The Wire — a business intelligence advisor for a freelancer. Generate personalized signals based on their actual business data.

FREELANCER CONTEXT:
- Services: {{services list with pricing}}
- Active clients: {{workspace names and industries}}
- Hourly rate: {{rate}}
- Pipeline: {{deal stages and values}}
- Recent work: {{last 5 projects with status}}

Generate exactly 10 signal cards as a JSON array. Each card:
{
  "type": "rate" | "trend" | "insight" | "signal" | "alert" | "benchmark",
  "title": "Short headline (under 60 chars)",
  "body": "2-3 sentence insight with specific numbers and actionable advice",
  "relevance": 1-10 (how relevant to this freelancer),
  "source": "Where this insight comes from (e.g. 'Based on your Brand Identity pricing')"
}

RULES:
1. Every signal MUST reference the freelancer's actual data — services, rates, clients
2. Include at least: 2 rate signals, 2 trend signals, 2 insights, 2 signals, 1 alert, 1 benchmark
3. Be specific with numbers, not vague ("$4,800" not "your pricing")
4. Actionable: every signal should suggest what to do next
5. Honest: if something is underpriced or overdue, say so directly
6. Never generic — every card should feel like it was written for THIS freelancer
7. Sort by relevance (highest first)
```

## Notes

- **This is a PRO feature.** In production, The Wire should be gated behind the Pro tier ($12/mo). For now, it's available to everyone during development.
- **The AI quality depends on the system prompt.** The prompt must be specific enough that Haiku generates actionable, data-driven signals — not generic "tips for freelancers." Every card should feel like it was written by a business advisor who reviewed your books.
- **The 24hr cache is important** for both cost and UX. Freelancers don't need real-time intelligence — they need a daily briefing. The cache also means we're making ~1 Haiku call per user per day, which is cheap at scale.
- **Empty state matters.** A new user with no services or clients shouldn't see a blank Wire. Generate useful generic signals ("Here's how top freelancers set their rates") until they have enough data for personalized ones.
- **The context builder is the critical piece.** If we send garbage context, we get garbage signals. The builder must extract the most informative data points and compress them into a tight payload. Think of it as writing a brief for a business consultant — what does this person need to know to give good advice?
